Current Research Interests

We explore the interplay between quantum optics and many-body physics, including open quantum systems theory and dissipative phase transitions. Our goal is to scalably engineer quantum correlations of light by exploiting the intrinsic many-body correlations in interacting emitters. The resulting bright quantum light will facilitate novel applications including quantum nonlinear spectroscopy, quantum metrology, extreme nonlinear quantum optics, and quantum communications.

Spin dynamics and its applications

We have investigated spin dynamics applications in several physical platforms, including solid-state spin systems and biological systems. One interesting example is the geomagnetic-field-assisted biocompass, in which the singlet–triplet dynamics of a radical-pair spin system determines the sensitivity to the geomagnetic field. We have clarified that magnetic noise fluctuations can functionalize the biocompass, resolving a key debate in the radical-pair mechanism of quantum magnetoreception.

Ultracold atoms scattering

A key advantage of ultracold atomic systems is the tunability of interatomic interactions, which are governed by the low-energy scattering length. We developed an efficient computational framework to evaluate scattering lengths while avoiding spurious numerical divergences in Green’s function calculations. This approach further incorporates long-range corrections and enables a multichannel generalization to describe inelastic scattering processes involving internal spin degrees of freedom.
